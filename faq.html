FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Find answers to common questions about our programmes, accreditation, pricing, and application process

Framework Alignment

Which cybersecurity frameworks are integrated into your programmes?

All IDR programmes are comprehensively aligned to industry-leading frameworks including:

NIST CSF
NCSC CAF
DORA
ISO 27001

This ensures graduates are immediately deployable across regulatory environments and can demonstrate compliance readiness from Day 1.

How do you ensure framework alignment remains current with regulatory updates?

Our curriculum undergoes quarterly review cycles with active CISOs and regulatory specialists. Framework updates are integrated within 60 days of publication, ensuring graduates are trained on the most current requirements. Live-fire scenarios reflect real-world implementation challenges across these frameworks.

Can I focus on specific frameworks relevant to my industry?

Yes. While all programmes cover core framework principles, we offer industry-specific tracks. Financial services emphasise DORA and PCI DSS. Healthcare focuses on HIPAA and HITECH. Government clients receive specialised NCSC CAF and Cabinet Office guidance. Corporate programmes can be customised to your specific regulatory environment.

Accreditation & Recognition

What academic recognition do IDR programmes carry?

IDR operates in partnership with Imperial College London, Royal Holloway, and UCL. Our 6-month Professional Diploma programmes carry academic credit equivalent to postgraduate level study. Executive Leadership programmes provide CPD certification recognised by major professional bodies including ISC2, ISACA, and BCS.

Are IDR qualifications recognised by employers internationally?

Absolutely. Our graduates hold leadership positions across tier-1 financial institutions, Big Four consultancies, and government agencies globally. The practical, framework-aligned approach ensures immediate workplace applicability. Many Fortune 500 companies specifically recruit from IDR cohorts due to the Day 1 readiness guarantee.

Can I use IDR qualifications toward professional certifications?

Yes. Our programmes provide structured CPE/CPD credits for CISSP, CISM, CISA, and other major certifications. The live-fire training methodology satisfies practical experience requirements for many certification bodies. We maintain formal relationships with certification providers to streamline the accreditation process.

Pricing & Investment

What is the investment for IDR programmes?

6-Month Professional Diplomas: £18,500 (includes all materials, live-fire lab access, career placement support)

2-Week Executive Leadership: £8,500 (intensive format for senior professionals)

Corporate programmes: Custom pricing based on cohort size and customisation requirements

All pricing includes VAT where applicable. Payment plans available.

Do you offer payment plans or employer sponsorship options?

Yes. Individual applicants can access 0% interest payment plans spread across programme duration. We work directly with employer Learning & Development budgets and maintain Apprenticeship Levy relationships. Many corporate partners integrate IDR programmes into their talent development pipelines with preferred pricing arrangements.

What is included in the programme investment?

Comprehensive programme investment includes:

  • Live-fire lab environment with enterprise-grade toolsets
  • 1:1 mentorship with active industry practitioners
  • Career placement support and network access
  • Lifetime access to IDR alumni community
  • Continuing education resources and framework updates
  • Professional development workshops and masterclasses

Programme Delivery

Are programmes delivered online or in-person?

Hybrid delivery model optimised for working professionals. Core lectures and live-fire exercises conducted via secure virtual environments. Intensive workshops and capstone projects delivered at our London and Mumbai facilities. Executive programmes include residential components for strategic simulation exercises and peer networking.

What time commitment is required for programme completion?

6-Month Diplomas: 15-20 hours per week (including live sessions, lab work, assignments)

Executive Leadership: Full-time intensive over 2 weeks (residential components)

Professional Development: Flexible scheduling, typically 5-8 hours per week

All programmes designed around working professional schedules with evening and weekend options.

Can I defer or transfer between programme cohorts?

Yes, with reasonable notice. We offer one complimentary deferral per application, subject to available capacity in future cohorts. Emergency deferrals considered on case-by-case basis. Corporate-sponsored candidates can transfer between related programmes within 12-month windows. Administrative fees may apply for multiple transfers.

Entry Requirements

What background is required for IDR programmes?

6-Month Diplomas: Bachelor's degree or equivalent professional experience. Technical aptitude assessment required.

Executive Leadership: 5+ years in senior management or strategy roles. P&L responsibility preferred but not mandatory.

Career Switcher Track: No prior cybersecurity experience required. Strong analytical background essential.

All candidates complete competency-based interview focusing on strategic thinking and leadership potential.

I don't have a technical background—can I still apply?

Absolutely. Our Career Switcher track is specifically designed for high-potential professionals transitioning from finance, consulting, law, and other strategic disciplines. Technical skills are developed progressively through our live-fire methodology. Many of our most successful graduates entered with zero cybersecurity experience but strong business acumen.

What is the application and selection process?

1. Initial Application: Career history, motivation statement, professional references

2. Technical Assessment: Problem-solving scenarios, analytical reasoning (non-specialist)

3. Strategic Interview: Leadership potential, decision-making approach, programme fit

4. Final Review: Holistic evaluation by admissions committee including industry practitioners

Typical process duration: 2-3 weeks from application to final decision

Corporate Bookings

Can we book exclusive cohorts for our organisation?

Yes. Corporate cohorts start from 12 participants and can be fully customised to your technology stack, regulatory environment, and strategic priorities. Delivery can be on-site at your facilities, at IDR centres, or hybrid. Curriculum adaptations include your specific use cases, threat landscape, and compliance requirements.

What corporate advisory services do you provide?

Fractional CISO Leadership: Part-time C-suite expertise (2-3 days/month minimum)

Talent Pipeline Development: Custom-trained candidates for your specific environment

AI Risk & Governance: Strategic deployment frameworks and compliance readiness

Crisis Simulation: Board-level incident response and communication training

All services delivered by active practitioners with current industry experience.

How do you ensure confidentiality for corporate programmes?

Comprehensive NDAs cover all faculty, participants, and materials. Corporate cohorts operate in isolated environments with organisation-specific scenarios and case studies. Sensitive materials are never shared across client boundaries. Our faculty hold active security clearances where required and understand enterprise confidentiality protocols.

Scholarships & Social Impact

What scholarship opportunities are available?

Diversity & Inclusion Scholarships: 50% fee reduction for underrepresented groups in cybersecurity

Social Mobility Programme: Full scholarships for high-potential candidates from disadvantaged backgrounds

Career Transition Support: Partial scholarships for professionals pivoting from other industries

Geographic Impact: Targeted support for candidates in Mumbai and emerging markets

20% of each cohort comprises scholarship recipients, ensuring diverse peer learning environments.

How do I apply for scholarship support?

Scholarship applications are integrated into the main application process—no separate forms required. Selection is need-blind during initial assessment, with financial support determined after programme acceptance. We Scholarship applications are integrated into the standard admissions process. Simply indicate interest on your application form and complete the additional impact statement (500 words maximum). Selection is merit-based, considering academic potential, professional trajectory, and commitment to cybersecurity leadership. Financial documentation may be required for need-based awards.

What ongoing support is provided to scholarship recipients?

Enhanced mentorship programmes with dedicated career coaching. Priority access to industry networking events and employer partnerships. Alumni mentor matching for ongoing professional development. Many scholarship recipients join our Social Impact Advisory Board, contributing to programme development and future candidate identification.
